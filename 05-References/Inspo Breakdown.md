# Inspo Breakdown — What & Why

---

## How to Use This File

Don't just collect screenshots. For every piece of inspiration, record:
1. **What:** The specific element you liked
2. **Why:** The design decision behind it
3. **How:** How Helixar could apply it differently

This turns passive inspiration into active learning.

---

## Landing Page — Hero Sections

### Raycast Hero
**What:** Dark background, subtle grid, orange/red logo, screenshot floating below copy
**Why:** The low-opacity grid creates depth without adding noise. The screenshot is large enough to be credible but not so large it crushes the copy.
**How Helixar applies it:** Same structure. But our grid is slightly more subtle (3% vs their ~5%), and our orange is warmer (#FF5C00 vs their red-orange). Our product screenshot shows more data to signal "this is a serious workspace tool."

### Linear Hero
**What:** "Linear is a better way to build software" — the directness of that headline
**Why:** It doesn't explain features. It makes a comparative claim. "Better" implies you already know the current way is worse.
**How Helixar applies it:** "Marketing used to need a team. Now it needs you." — same directness, different claim. We're not saying "better marketing" — we're saying "different category entirely."

### Vercel Hero
**What:** Very little copy. Massive headline. One CTA.
**Why:** When your product has brand recognition, you can afford restraint. The negative space around the headline creates confidence.
**How Helixar applies it:** We're not Vercel yet — we need more explanation. But we should not be afraid of big type and generous whitespace. Most marketing SaaS landing pages are too busy.

## App UI — Sidebars

### Linear Sidebar
**What:** Icon + label nav, subtle active state, workspace switcher at top
**Why:** The active state is nearly invisible — just a very slight background — but it's perfectly clear. They don't shout "you are here."
**How Helixar applies it:** Same restraint, but we use orange for the active state (our brand color earns its place here). One moment of orange per view maximum.

### Raycast Extension List
**What:** Dense list with icons, hover states, keyboard hints
**Why:** High information density that doesn't feel cluttered because of consistent vertical alignment and proper type hierarchy.
**How Helixar applies it:** Research list view, campaign list view — same density principle.

## Motion — References

### Stripe's Payment Animation
**What:** Numbers rolling up when you hit their pricing section
**Why:** It makes a static number feel earned. The user watches it count to the final value and it creates a micro-moment of investment.
**How Helixar applies it:** On our Compound Advantage section — animate "10–20 hours" counting down to "minutes."

### Vercel Deployment Animation
**What:** Build steps appearing one by one, each line fading in
**Why:** It shows the system working. It makes the abstract (deployment) feel concrete.
**How Helixar applies it:** Research loading screen — each platform scraping step appears and checks off as it completes. Makes the AI feel like it's actually working, not just spinning.

## Typography — References

### Geist on Vercel's site
**What:** Large, tightly tracked headlines at negative letter-spacing
**Why:** At large sizes, default tracking creates too much gap between letters. Tightening it (negative letter-spacing) makes headlines feel more confident and designed.
**How Helixar applies it:** All headlines at text-3xl and above get -0.04em to -0.06em tracking. Already defined in our type system.

## Empty States — References

### Linear's "No Issues" screen
**What:** Tiny icon, brief heading, one-sentence explanation, no CTA
**Why:** Not every empty state needs a CTA. Sometimes "nothing here" just means "nothing here yet." The lack of urgency creates calm.
**How Helixar applies it:** Memory empty state — "Memory builds as you use Helixar." No CTA. Just a statement of what will happen.

### Notion's template gallery empty state
**What:** "Get started with a template" when a page is empty
**Why:** Turns emptiness into an opportunity. The empty state knows what you were trying to do and offers a shortcut.
**How Helixar applies it:** Campaign empty state offers "Start from a template" — pre-built campaign structures for common use cases (product launch, brand awareness, etc.)
