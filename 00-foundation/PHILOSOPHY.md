## The one rule

If you can remove it and the design still works — **remove it.**

---

## What the reference board is saying

**Catch Conference poster.**
Three colors. Black, white, orange. Orange appears ONCE — on the cube. The type is
architecture. "CATCH CO__MUNITY CONFERENCE 2020" at massive scale is a load-bearing wall.
The layout leans against it. The negative space below isn't emptiness — it's breathing room.

**AVIR slide.**
Near-black background. White type so large it escapes the frame. Three geometric shapes.
That's everything. It commands respect because it refuses to decorate itself.

**7.N/ · 0 spread.**
Violent black-and-white contrast. One orange horizontal block. One. That is the accent.
The rest is black type colliding with white space.

**lab.co.**
Off-white background. Massive Geist type. One orange rectangle used as a physical block,
not a color. A human photo. Nothing else. Reads like expensive print design.

**The Sundash board.**
Black, orange, grey. Playing with type weight and spacing. The orange is used in gradients
AND as flat fills — but always as a material, never decoratively sprinkled.

**What they all share:**
Restraint. Confidence. The orange earns its place by being surrounded by discipline.

---

## The three colors

Black. White. Orange. That is the entire palette.

The orange is NOT a highlight. It is NOT for links or hover states or decorative dots.
It is a **material** — a solid block, a stamp, a physical object on the page.
Treat it like ink on newsprint, not like a CSS variable someone sprinkled around.

The moment you add a fourth color — a green checkmark, a blue link, a teal badge —
you have broken the tension that makes the design work.

---

## What this is NOT

- Not a generic AI SaaS dark-mode tool
- Not a startup landing page with 15 feature cards
- Not a Notion template with ✓ tick lists
- Not "modern" in the way that means nothing
- Not decorated

---

## The litmus test

Show it to a CMO for 3 seconds.
They should feel: *"This is a serious tool. These people understand my world."*

Not: *"Oh, another AI thing."*
Not: *"This looks like their engineer built the website."*
Not: *"Cool animations though."*

---

## Left-align everything

Centered brutalist type is an oxymoron.
Every headline. Every label. Every command block.
Left. Always.

---

## The negative space rule

Every section needs at least 40% of its height to be intentional emptiness.
If your content fills the viewport — you have too much in one section. Cut.
The emptiness is not a layout problem. It is the design.

---

## Typography is architecture

A 100px Bebas Neue headline is not showing off.
It is a structural column the rest of the layout leans against.
Remove it and the section collapses.
That is how you know it belongs.
