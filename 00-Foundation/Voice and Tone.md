# Voice & Tone — Helixar

---

## The Writing Principle

Write like someone who has been in the room. Who knows the pain firsthand. Who doesn't need to explain why marketing is hard — because they already know you know.

**Not:** "Helixar leverages AI to streamline your marketing workflows."
**Yes:** "You already know what good marketing looks like. You just need more hours in the day."

---

## Voice (Always True)

These are fixed. They don't change by context.

- **Direct.** No preamble. Lead with the point.
- **Confident.** Never hedge. Never "might", "could", "sort of."
- **Human.** We talk to people, not personas.
- **Earned.** We don't claim — we show. Then let the reader conclude.

---

## Tone (Shifts by Context)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page hero | Bold, punchy, urgent | "The whole marketing team. None of the overhead." |
| Feature explanation | Clear, matter-of-fact | "Helixar pulls all platforms simultaneously — not one by one." |
| Empty states (in app) | Warm, inviting | "Nothing here yet. Start by researching a competitor." |
| Error messages | Honest, helpful | "That didn't work. Here's what to try next." |
| Onboarding | Encouraging, brief | "You're set up. Here's where most people start." |
| Tooltips | Precise, no fluff | "Orange = requires your approval before anything goes live." |

---

## Words We Use

- **Workspace** (not platform, tool, app)
- **Intelligence** (not data, analytics)
- **Compounds** (not improves, gets better)
- **Loop** (not workflow, process)
- **You** (always second person — never "users" or "marketers" in UI copy)
- **Research, Analyze, Generate, Track** (the four verbs of Helixar)

---

## Words We Never Use

- Leverage
- Streamline
- Powerful
- Robust
- Seamless
- Game-changer
- Revolutionary
- Next-generation
- Solution

---

## Sentence Structure Rules

1. **Short sentences hit harder.** Use them for key claims.
2. **Long sentences are for nuance.** Use them for explanations.
3. **Never bury the verb.** Action first, qualifier second.
4. **Parallel structure in lists.** Every bullet starts with a verb or a noun — never mix.
5. **One idea per sentence.** Split anything with two commas.

---

## The Gut Check

Read it out loud. If you'd feel embarrassed saying it to a smart colleague — rewrite it.

If it sounds like a press release — rewrite it.

If it could be on any other marketing product's website — rewrite it.

Helixar copy should sound like it could only be Helixar.
